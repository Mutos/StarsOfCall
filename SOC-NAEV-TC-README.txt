|===============================================================|
|			Background : the Hoshikaze 2250 Universe			|
|===============================================================|
|																|
The night sky always draws our eyes. People look up with a question, or in wonder, and sometimes with a dream: to venture forth among the stars.

The first spacefaring species was the pacifist, arthropoid K'Rinns. After two tragic events that occurred while they explored their homeworld, H'Rkann, they definitely knew they were not alone in a universe full of danger. So they set out to space and gradually explored the nearby stellar systems, slowly but surely building a true spacefaring civilisation.

Their first trauma, back in their cradleworld, was the Army of Madk'rinns, an unprecedented historical episode where otherwise ordinary K'Rinns were turned into ruthless killers by drugs and hypnosis-like propaganda. After this episode, all known k'rinn city-states banded together to form the K'Rinn Council and set out to explore their homeworld, H'Rkann, so as not to get caught by surprise, would another mad people exist somewhere else.

During the exploration, they encountered a cousin species, the Ha'Tinkar, from a remote continent. A golden era of cultural intercourse ensued, but it ended in an unexpected tragedy. The Ha'Tinkar had been genetically altered millennia before by alien settlers, only known as the Til'kk'Rantk, the "Flying Gods", who stayed on H'Rkann only a few decades. Sadly, their hasty gengineering work left the Ha'Tinkar with a sensitivity to a family of diseases. Among them was an otherwise innocuous virus endemic in K'Rinn population. Once fortuitous contact was made, they were doomed and dwindled to extinction in a mere half-century, decimated by an atrocious illness, despite all the help K'Rinns could gather.

The Rithai were second to set out in space. These warlike, feline centaurs have been dominated for centuries by multiple warring powers. In a manner of decades, two powerful Clans emerged : Irmothem and Arythem vying for domination of Skarae, the rith homeworld. After a few centuries of latent conflict, as unaligned people were slowly being crushed between the two blocks, they banded together to create Clan Irilia, which set out to conquer the planet of the same name. They schemed to obtain the agreement of the main Clans, but the main expedition was nearly destroyed upon liftoff, when the main Clans betrayed their oath of neutrality upon it. In a civilisation built on honor, that act changed the Irilia attitude towards remaining Skarae Clans to relentless hostility.

The three main Clans raced for the stars, first with sublight colony ships, then with hyperjumps when they became available. The Rith Sphere was growing at a fast pace when they encountered the K'Rinns.

At that time, the fledgling Human Sphere was split between two powers. From a dying, resources-depleted Earth had risen the Political Corporations, aka Policorpos, which have used the infinite resources of space to overcome failing earthbound nationstates. They settled Mars and set out to establish colonies around nearby stars, then discovered hyperjump.

What was thought as a means to unite a mankind scattered among the stars proved instead a seed of discorde, thanks to the greed of the Policorpos. The clash between the sublight colonies and the Policorpos that sought to reconquer them by force gave birth to the Planetarist Alliance, at first an ideology claiming political independence for every world.

The dissent was further fueled by a dirty, bloody conflict among the Policorpos over the remnants of an extinct species, whose cradleworld was a hoard of technological treasures. First named Ashioto (jap. "Footprints"), it soon became known as Purgatory as the conflict expanded and became more and more horrible and Policorpos began using advanced techs without proper understanding, causing catastrophic biohazard events. Later, the Purgatorians were proved to be the Til'kk'Rantk whose bugged gengineering doomed the Ha'Tinkar. The numerous renegades from all Purgatory War factions growed until they overwhelmed their former armies and made an end to the conflict. They later became the core of the Alliance's army.

Soon after the Renegades Army joined the Planetarists, the new State established itself out of Corporate reach, on the newly settled world of Sanctuary. In the face of this new threat, the Policorpos reluctantly united to form a loose counter-alliance, they named Cartel Sol.

Cartelist forces sought to resettle a metal-rich pocket of systems they abandoned a few years ago. They were faced by the miners, who in the meantime did turn their asteroid-mining ships into fighters and the ore-gathering megacargoes into Fortresses. Calling themselves "Hoshi no Hekka", meaning the Star Lords, they sided with the Alliance against Cartel Sol as an independant State.

Meanwhile, the meeting between K'Rinns and Rithai led to several short-lived alliances, which finally ended when the Arythem-Irilia wars extended to k'rinn space. Planet Irilia-12 had Irilia and Arythem settlements and was ruled under an interClans treaty, with the help of K'Rinn Council diplomacy. When war erupted upon a disputed ore-rich region, it soon spread out and engulfed the K'Rinn orbital habitat. The Arythem were the aggresors, but the k'rinn retaliation and the First Fringe War that ensued were a surprise to them, as the K'Rinn-Irilia alliance was far stronger than they thought. This war ended in the Uneasy Peace, with Irilia and the K'Rinn Council as winners, Clan Arythem left to ruminate vengeance and Clan Irmothem going into further isolation from the ongoing cold war. During this period, culture soared among the Irilia and K'Rinn people and the base book of Xenoethnology, "Of Species and Behaviors" was published by Eorath ud Orthid-Irilia.

The Second Fringe War was a severe blow to Irilia power and soon it became evident that, even with k'rinn support, the Clan could not bear a staying conflict. K'Rinn space itself became under attack. All seemed lost for Irilia and the Coucil when, by pure luck, a human ship jumped in the middle of a local skirmish. Attacked by Arythem forces, its return fire proved devastating and took Arythem commanders by surprise. Following this battle, the Planetarist Alliance quickly entered the war as part of an exchange with the K'Rinn-Irilia alliance.

However, rather than being forced into an alliance with another non-k'rinn faction, the colony of Ka'Ralt declared secession from the Council and implemented a last-ditch plan : gengineering a new Army of Madk'rinns to save their world. That plan had been turned down by mainstream k'rinn instances, and from that instant, Ka'Ralters were considered as non-k'rinns and traitors to the spirit of their species.

Thanks to k'rinn technology, Planetarists were soon able to launch the invasion of the Sol system. They captured Mars, then Earth, and the last Cartelists brass fled in a barely completed, untested prototype battleship. Backed with Planetarist supplies, the Council forced the Arythem offensive to a halt, then to retreat. The winning States and their opponent formed the Commonwealth of Spacegoing Species, to prevent any future conflict.

While the war seemed to turn in favor of the Council-Irilia-Planetarists coalition, an Arythem destroyer encountered a strange ship, seemingly alive and dying in an isolated star system. The sole surviving crew membrer was brought to Skarae. So, Ranger-among-the-Stars-7 became the first Pupeteer to encounter another sentient species. But, upon watching his ability to attach to an animal and control its nervous system, some Arythem official saw a possible weapon against Irilia. Despite having strongly expressed his disgust at controlling sentients, the worse crime ever for Pupeteers and a nauseating act for the perpetrator, the prisoner was infiltrated into Irilia High Command and forced to dictate acts of treatise to unwilling high-ranking officers. After enduring this for months, he managed to escaped and reveal the truth to Arythem highest authorities, who executed the culprits. At last avenged, but unable to bear the atrocities he has been forced to do, Ranger-among-the-Stars-7 committed suicide.

With such a background, the first official contact between Rithai and Pupeteers was at least difficult. Some time was needed before Pupeteers could believe the apologies they were served. But for the Arythem, it was a question of honour. The Clan had been dishonoured, so they insisted and an agreement was finally reached.

From now on and despite deep cultural differences, Pupeteers are taking a growing influence in the Commonwealth. Pacifists and empaths at heart, they are sometimes unwilling to trust beings who kill and eat animals, as much as other species are reticent to trust people who control animals and derive their bloodstream for sustenance. But from both sides, good faith and a willingness to communicate allow constant progress to be made.

Pupeteers also came with some knowledge of a mysterious species, the Navigators, which, like them, uses living starships. Navigators are still a mystery, but thanks to pupeteer researches, we now know a little more about them, their very advanced, miles-long living ships, their uncanny ability to jump without natural jump-points and the 3 "recreational worlds" they seem to possess and jealously guard.

However, from deep within the Planetarist Alliance, that now ruled most of the Human Sphere, turmoil has arisen. Earth and Mars, as well as some First Colonies and a few worlds with a strong corporatist past, rebelled against the authority of Sanctuary. For them, the Mother World had an eternal, almost religious right to govern all Humans. Tension was rising and between riots, demonstrations, skirmishes and threats of secession of several worlds, it seemed a new war would soon engulf human space.

Seeing that, the K'Rinn Council quickly stepped in and offered mediation. An agreement was reached over a peaceful secession and a new State took place among the Commonwealth : the Core League, comprising Sol and most of the rebellious colonies.

A few years later, contact was made with a remnant of Cartel Sol, the Purple Trail. Descendants of Cartelists who fled on the "Purple Flag" when Sol was taken by Planetarists, they settled on a faraway mining outpost, survived its harsh environment and rebuild a State in its own right.

During its time of isolation, the Purple Trail had made first contact with another sentient species, the reptilo-avian Sshaads, already known to Rithai and K'Rinns but with no connexion to human space, and developed a network of influence that enabled them to re-enter the Human Sphere without the Alliance being able to oppose.

When the Sshaads were invited to join the Commonwealth, some States were reticent. No co-colonies could be made between Sshaads and other species because of a peculiarity of sshaad biology. Young Sshaad are not sentient beings, but pack predators. They do not attack adults, except in extreme conditions, but Sshaad settlements are dangerous for non-sshaads, who are easily seen by immature Sshaads as running breakfasts.

Recently, though, the Sshaad Sphere is being shaken by a new political movement, involving a newly contacted species, the Shapeshifters. This otherwise pacifist species, living in sentient Hives from which One-Spirit individuals can detach to live freely, learned violence the hard way, when their cradleworld was invaded by the Baartish, a sshaad colonization corpostate.

The first sshaad strike let them without their satellite network and prompted them to hide their presence on their own world. The settlers soon discovered they were not alone. Some denied sentience to the Shapeshifters, while other began to cooperate and built common villages. Luckily, the two species share a similar way of extending their territories, and a new way of life began to emerge among the colonists villages and nearby Hives.

But for extremists of both sides, this was treason. As Shapeshifter Hives easily exchange memories, this initial reaction was quickly overcome on their side, but it expanded among Sshaads, fuelled by warlike and xenophobic factions like the Shodens. The doctrine of Complementarism that formed between the two species has now risen as a fracture point in sshaad space, dividing factions and giving rise to strong tensions.

So, here's my view, in very broad terms, of the history behind our year 2250. I'm a Spacer and, as you probably know, we Spacers from different species often share more among us than with Worlders (I wouldn't call you "Gravvies" or "Dirtballers", 'cause you might resent it) of the same species. So, if you really wanna become one of us, let's take off !
